# 03 - World Layout

---

# Purpose

This document defines the physical environment of the portfolio.

The world should feel like a believable studio apartment belonging to an AI/ML engineer and full-stack developer.

The environment should communicate personality before the visitor interacts with anything.

The room should never feel like a museum or exhibition.

It should feel lived in.

---

# World Structure

Version 1 consists of two connected spaces.

1. Main Studio
2. Recreation Room

The Main Studio contains all professional portfolio information.

The Recreation Room contains hobbies and personal interests.

---

# Studio Theme

The studio combines:

- Living Room
- Workspace
- Study Area

into a single open environment.

It should feel cozy, modern and slightly futuristic.

Not cyberpunk.

Not realistic.

Not cartoonish.

Think of a modern indie game.

---

# Room Philosophy

The player should NOT see the entire room immediately.

The room should be larger than the camera viewport.

Visitors discover different sections naturally by turning and walking.

Every direction should reveal something interesting.

---

# Studio Zones

The studio is divided into functional zones.

These are not separate rooms.

They are areas inside one large studio.

---

# Zone 1

Entrance

Purpose

Spawn point.

Contains:

- Entrance door
- Shoe rack
- Welcome mat
- Small plant

No interactable portfolio objects.

This area simply welcomes the visitor.

---

# Zone 2

Center Area

Purpose

Relaxation space.

Contains:

- Sofa
- Coffee table
- Rug
- Floor lamp

This acts as the visual center of the apartment.

It separates all other zones.

# Lighting

Natural sunlight enters through windows.

Warm indoor lighting fills darker areas.

Interactive objects should have subtle accent lighting.

Avoid excessive neon lighting.

The room should feel comfortable.

---

# Decorations

Plants

Wall clock

Coffee mug

Backpack

Notebook

Sticky notes

Headphones

Picture frames

Curtains

Rug

Desk accessories

The goal is making the studio believable.

---

# Camera Visibility

At no point should the visitor see every portfolio object simultaneously.

Turning the camera should reveal new sections naturally.

The apartment should reward exploration.

---

# Navigation

The player is free to move anywhere.

No invisible barriers except walls.

No forced paths.

No teleportation.

Movement should feel natural.

---
