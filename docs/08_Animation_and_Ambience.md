# 08 - Animation and Ambience

---

# Purpose

This document defines every environmental animation, lighting effect,
particle effect, and audio cue that contributes to immersion.

The environment should never feel static.

Even when the visitor is standing still,
the world should feel alive.

---

# Design Philosophy

Animations should not exist to impress.

Animations should make the world believable.

Everything should have purpose.

The visitor should almost forget that animations are happening.

---

---

# Environmental Behaviour

The room should never freeze.

Even while idle,
something should always be moving.

Examples

Curtains move slightly

Plants sway gently

Clock ticks

Computer fans rotate

Projector standby light blinks

Desk lamp emits a subtle flicker

Coffee steam rises

Dust particles float

Nothing should remain perfectly still.

---

# Lighting

Lighting should guide attention.

Do not use arrows.

Do not use giant icons.

Instead

Bright objects naturally attract the eye.

---

# Ambient Lighting

Warm sunlight

Soft room lighting

Desk lamp

Projector glow

Computer monitor glow

Phone screen glow

Window light

---

# Dynamic Lighting

Interactive objects may temporarily change lighting.

Examples

Projector

Room becomes slightly darker.

Projection becomes brighter.

Phone

Phone screen illuminates nearby desk.

Bookshelf

Shelf receives warm spotlight.

Notice Board

Reading lamp turns on.

The room should help direct attention.

---

# Particle Effects

Particles should be subtle.

Examples

Floating dust

Window light particles

Tiny floating motes

Occasional sparkle near projector

Very soft particles around globe

Avoid excessive visual effects.

---

# Environmental Audio

The room should always have ambient sound.

Examples

Soft room ambience

Computer fan

Clock ticking

Wind outside

Birds

Rain (future weather)

Electrical hum

Nothing should be silent.

---

# Footsteps

Walking produces footsteps.

Different surfaces should sound different.

Examples

Wood

Rug

Tile

Footsteps should stop immediately when standing still.

---

# Object Audio

Every object should have unique sound.

Examples

Projector

Fan

Startup click

Phone

Unlock sound

Notification sound

Bookshelf

Book sliding

Page turning

Globe

Soft rotation

Door

Opening

Closing

Laptop

Lid opening

Keyboard typing

Gaming Console

Power button

Startup chime

These sounds should be subtle.

---

# Camera Audio

Movement should synchronize with

Footsteps

Object sounds

Environmental ambience

Everything should feel connected.

---

# Recreation Room

The recreation room should feel different.

Professional ambience should reduce.

The room should feel warmer.

Relaxed.

Personal.

---

# Background Music

Very subtle.

Instrumental.

Lo-fi.

Ambient.

Volume should remain low.

The music should never compete with interactions.

---

# Transition Philosophy

Never rely on

Fade to black

Slide animations

Page transitions

Everything should transition through

Movement

Lighting

Camera

Object animation

Sound

---

# Performance

Animations should remain lightweight.

Target

Stable frame rate

No stuttering

Minimal CPU usage

Animations should prioritize smoothness over complexity.

---

# Success Criteria

A visitor standing still for one minute should still feel that the room is alive.

The environment should quietly communicate warmth, professionalism, and personality without overwhelming the visitor.
